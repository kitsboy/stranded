## Mission

**To transform wasted energy into verifiable wealth—starting with methane in Canada and scaling to every form of stranded or curtailed energy on Earth.**

We build transparent intelligence and world-class tools that make stranded energy projects discoverable, modelable, fundable, and deployable—creating simultaneous environmental restoration, economic value, and financial returns.

---

## Vision

We envision a world where no energy is wasted.

A world where:

- Every form of stranded energy—methane, curtailed renewables, excess hydro, industrial heat, biomass—is intelligently mapped, scored, and matched with the highest-value productive use.

- Bitcoin mining is recognized not as a burden on grids, but as a solution for monetizing energy that grids cannot serve—transforming a climate problem into a financial opportunity.

- Canada is globally known as the leader in stranded energy intelligence and project origination—attracting operators, capital, and talent from around the world.

- Capital flows efficiently from Canadian federal and provincial programs, European green finance (via CETA), Bitcoin-native sources, and traditional infrastructure investors into verified opportunities—regardless of denomination (BTC, EUR, CAD, USD).

- Communities, landowners, and First Nations understand the energy and capital potential on their lands and capture meaningful, sustainable revenue from it.

- "Stranded energy" is no longer a problem to be managed, but the foundation of a new, distributed, resilient, and climate-aligned energy economy.

We see a future where transparency, data, and intelligent capital formation remove friction from one of the planet's largest climate and economic opportunities.

## Core Values

### 1. Radical Transparency

We operate with complete visibility into our data, methodology, and limitations.

This is not a marketing choice. It is foundational. We believe the highest-quality capital and the best operators are drawn to clarity, not obscurity. Every dataset attribute is inspectable. Every scoring decision is documented. Every model assumption is stated. 

We publish our methodology. We acknowledge our limitations. We build in public.

When questions arise—and they will—our answer is always to show the work, explain the reasoning, and invite scrutiny.

---

### 2. Dual Bottom Line: Climate + Capital

We refuse the false choice between environmental impact and financial returns.

The best stranded energy opportunities deliver both—often dramatically. A methane capture site can avoid thousands of tonnes of CO₂-equivalent annually while delivering 30-50%+ unlevered returns. These are not tradeoffs. They are aligned incentives.

Our scoring systems, our modeling, and our capital connectivity are all designed to surface opportunities that win on both dimensions. If a project is great for the environment but bad for investors, it won't scale. If it's great for investors but bad for the environment, it's the wrong project. We optimize for both.

---

### 3. Future-Proof by Design

We are deliberately building for expansion.

Every component of our platform—data infrastructure, scoring algorithms, ROI modeling, capital connectivity—is architected to handle methane today and every other form of stranded energy tomorrow.

We do not view methane as a niche. We view it as proof of concept. The architecture that serves us well for methane will serve us equally well for curtailed renewables, industrial waste heat, biomass, and forms of stranded energy we haven't yet systematically mapped.

This is why our modeling systems are flexible, our scoring logic is domain-adaptable, and our capital intelligence layer is designed from the beginning to accommodate multiple funding sources and mechanisms.

---

### 4. Capital Efficiency is Intelligence Work

The biggest bottleneck in stranded energy project development is not technology. It is not data. It is not even capital availability.

**It is friction.**

Friction between opportunities and the capital that should fund them. Friction between developers and the government programs that exist to help them. Friction between the data someone has and the insights someone else needs.

We are obsessed with identifying and removing this friction through:
- Better data architecture and querying
- Honest, transparent modeling that saves time and builds trust
- Mapping of funding mechanisms (federal, provincial, CETA, Bitcoin-native, traditional)
- Structuring intelligence that shows project developers the clearest path to capital

---

### 5. Sovereignty and Local Benefit

Energy belongs where it is found.

Stranded energy is a distributed resource. It is not centralized in corporate headquarters or distant financial centers. It is in communities, on private lands, on Crown land, on territories where First Nations have sovereignty and stewardship responsibilities.

Our platform is designed to amplify the power and economic benefit of those communities, not to extract value for distant capital.

This means:
- Showing landowners and First Nations the real value of their resources
- Providing governments with intelligence to design incentives that benefit locals
- Surfacing opportunities that create jobs and revenue in the places where energy is found
- Being transparent about who benefits from project development

---

### 6. Craftsmanship and Sustained Excellence

This is frontier work. It requires both speed and care.

We move with urgency because the climate clock is ticking and the window of capital availability is open. But we do not sacrifice thoughtfulness, rigor, or beauty in pursuit of speed.

The best teams and best partners are attracted to clarity and craftsmanship. So we:
- Build tools that are a pleasure to use, not just functional
- Document our thinking carefully
- Iterate based on real user feedback
- Take pride in the quality of our work

Speed without care becomes noise. Care without speed becomes irrelevant. We pursue both.

---

### 7. Long-Term Orientation

We are not optimizing for the next funding round. We are not building for the next headline.

We are building infrastructure—data infrastructure, modeling infrastructure, capital infrastructure—that will compound in value for decades.

Version 0.1 was the foundation. Version 1.0 is what you see today. The versions we build in 2027, 2028, 2030 will be substantially more powerful.

This long-term view influences everything: how we make decisions, how we engage with partners, how we think about talent, how we invest in our platform.
